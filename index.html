THE MECHANISMS UNDERLYING SEA LEVEL RISE

Sea level rise will occur because land-borne ice sheets will melt, adding water to the oceans, and because warming seawater will expand. An educational dynamic histogram graphic will be constructed to visually depict these effects.

OCEAN DEPTH HISTROGRAM

The graph above shows how much room there is on Earth for water a function of elevation. The horizontal line represents sea-level today. At present, some water does exists above the horizontal line because the land supports rivers, lakes, mountain glaciers, and polar ice. As the ice melts, the oceans will rise. As ocean temperatures increase, the volume of the water in the oceans will also increase. Both effects will combine to accelerate coastal flooding. A considerable percentage of our land area is low-lying and therefore at risk.
